Assessment

In every client conversation, you will be recommending a space — not just a brand. To do that well, you need to know two things: where the space sits (the building) and who operates it (the brand).

This chapter gives you the vocabulary and framework to assess any coworking or flex operator. By the end, you should be able to walk into any coworking center, assess the operator, understand their product mix, and position the right solution for the right client.

01

Start with the Building, Not the Operator

Before understanding a coworking brand, always understand the building. The building is the foundation of every workspace decision. It determines accessibility, occupier profile, employee experience, parking availability, infrastructure quality, and long-term value.

These are the nine things you need to know about any building before evaluating the operator inside it:

A premium operator in a poor building may struggle, while a good operator in a landmark Grade A building can be highly attractive to clients. The building sets the context — the operator fills it.

Brand Division — Who Operates the Space?

Flex operators can broadly be divided into Premium and Economical categories. This distinction affects everything: the building they occupy, the clients they attract, the design quality, and the price point.

Premium Operators
Examples

03

Product Division — What Does the Brand Offer?

Every coworking or flex operator offers a set of products designed for different customer segments. Understanding the product mix helps you identify the right solution for each client before you ever walk them into a center.

Coworking Products

Flexible solutions for individuals, startups, and small teams. These are entry-level products that require little or no commitment.

Hot Desk
Access to any available desk in a shared area. No fixed seat — ideal for those who don't need a permanent setup or work irregular hours.
Dedicated Desk
A fixed desk reserved exclusively for the member. Your desk, your monitor, your setup — just inside a shared floor.
Private Cabin
A private enclosed office within the coworking center. Door, privacy, and your branding — with access to all shared common infrastructure.
Day Pass
Workspace access for a single day. Useful for visiting professionals, freelancers, or teams needing occasional space.
Virtual Office
Business address and GST/company registration services without physical occupancy. Used by businesses that need a credible address without daily on-site operations.

Enterprise Products

Customized solutions for larger organizations. These require longer commitments and often involve design and build involvement from the operator.

Managed Office
A fully serviced and managed private office. The operator designs, furnishes, and operates the space on the client's behalf — the client just moves in.
Built-to-Suit
Office designed and customized entirely to the client's specifications — layout, branding, and infrastructure built from scratch for that client.
Enterprise Suites
Dedicated office spaces for larger teams requiring private, branded floors or wings within a flex center.
HQ Solutions
Headquarters-grade office solutions for companies wanting a flagship office with premium address, full amenities, and enterprise-class fit-out.
Campus Solutions
Multi-floor or multi-building campus arrangements for large organizations with high headcount requirements across a single location.

Meeting & Event Products

Bookable spaces available on demand. You don't need to be a member to access most of these — they're sold by the hour or by the day.

Meeting Rooms
Small rooms for 4–8 people. Ideal for client calls, interviews, and quick team syncs. Most centers charge per hour.
Conference Rooms
Larger rooms for 10–20 people. Used for board presentations, client pitches, and half-day workshops.
Board Rooms
Premium rooms for senior leadership meetings. Often equipped with full AV systems, formal seating, and catering options.
Training Rooms
Classroom-style setup for onboarding sessions, training programs, and learning events. Often have whiteboard walls, projectors, and rearrangeable seating.
Event Spaces
Open, flexible areas for large-format gatherings — product launches, networking events, town halls, and corporate offsites.

Parking Assessment

Is parking available in the building?
Is it basement parking or surface parking?
What is the parking ratio — how many slots per seat?
Are there nearby parking solutions if building parking is limited?
Parking is one of the most commonly raised client objections in Gurugram. Always know the parking situation before recommending a center. Clients with large teams or car-dependent employees will disqualify a center on parking alone — even if everything else is perfect.

Amenities Assessment

Meeting Room & Conference Room Hours

How many meeting room hours are included per seat per month?
Are hours shared across the team or per individual?
What is the rate for additional hours beyond the included quota?
Do unused hours roll over to the next month?
06

Pricing Assessment

Never take the first quoted price at face value. Always break it down — what's included, what's excluded, and what the real monthly cost looks like for your client.

Check the quoted price per seat / cabin.
Verify which amenities are included in the price.
Identify any exclusions from the package.
Understand all additional costs — parking, meeting rooms, printing, lockers, etc.
Confirm the final all-inclusive monthly cost.
