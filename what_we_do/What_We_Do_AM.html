01

What is Assisted Marketplace?

Assisted Marketplace is myHQ's consultative solution for companies looking for office space. Most companies looking for an office have never done it before, don't know the market, and don't have the time to visit dozens of properties. Our consultants assist the client in finding the perfect office solution tailored to their unique needs — across coworking, managed office, or conventional leasing — instead of leaving them to navigate a fragmented, broker-driven market alone.

Key benefits of myHQ:

  • End to End Office Consultant: one dedicated point of contact owns the search from brief to move-in — the client isn't bounced between a broker for the space, a lawyer for the agreement, and an interior team for the fit-out.
  • Zero Brokerage: clients don't pay us a brokerage fee. This removes the single biggest reason companies are wary of going through an intermediary in the first place.
  • Faster Decisions, Smooth Move-ins: a structured process compresses decision time and de-risks the move — clients aren't second-guessing themselves three weeks into a search because they have no benchmark for what "a good deal" looks like.
  • Best Negotiated Prices: our scale and market knowledge get clients better commercial terms than they'd get alone, since we run this volume of transactions across the same set of landlords and operators repeatedly.

Target audience spans three segments, each with different urgency and decision-making style:

  • Startups: early and growth stage companies. These clients move fast, often need flexibility built into the deal (shorter lock-ins), and are usually choosing coworking.
  • Mid-Size Companies: growing businesses, typically 20–100 employees. These clients are often deciding between coworking and a managed office as headcount stabilises.
  • Large Businesses: larger businesses with a stable headcount. These clients have the scale and predictability to justify a managed office or a full conventional lease.
AM = expert consultant + zero brokerage + best negotiated prices. The client doesn't need to know the market — that's what we're here for.

02

AM Solutions for Clients

AM offers three distinct office products, each suited to a different stage and style of company:

Coworking Spaces
Your own reserved desk in a shared, vibrant coworking environment. Open desks (1–100) or cabins (4–100 seater). Dealing in # of seats.
Managed Office
A fully serviced, custom-built office — designed, furnished, and run for you so your team moves in faster. Generally for 50+ team size. Dealing in # of seats/sqft.
Conventional Leasing
Long-term leased spaces for companies needing full control & flexibility. Generally for 150+ team size (5,000 sqft+). Dealing in sqft.

The three products sit on a spectrum of control versus convenience. Coworking trades the lowest control for the highest convenience — the client doesn't manage facilities, security, or interiors at all, they just occupy seats. Managed Office sits in the middle — the client gets a space built and run to their brand and layout, but myHQ's operator still handles the day-to-day running of it. Conventional Leasing gives the client full control of the space (their own fit-out, their own facilities management) in exchange for taking on more of the operational and compliance burden themselves.

This is also why the pricing unit changes as you move along that spectrum: coworking is priced per seat because the operator is selling occupancy, not square footage; managed office blends seats and sqft because the build is customised to headcount; and conventional leasing is priced purely in sqft because the client is taking the whole floor or building, regardless of how many seats they eventually fit into it.

Know your client's stage and headcount — that tells you which solution fits before you even show them options. Coworking = seats, Managed = seats/sqft, Leasing = sqft.

03

End-to-End Consulting in 6 Steps

We provide end-to-end consulting at each step, in ALL transactions. Each step has its own checklist of factors a consultant must evaluate before moving the client forward:

  • 1. Strategic Discovery: Identify potential office space through city and micro-market analysis. Factors: talent pool, competitors, traffic, cost of living. This is where you narrow down the right city and even the right neighbourhood before a single property is shown — getting this wrong means every later step has to be redone.
  • 2. Curated Shortlist: Evaluate and benchmark office space to create a shortlist that can be visited. Factors: budget, connectivity, kind of assets, experience at the centre. The goal is a tight shortlist the client can actually visit in a day or two, not an overwhelming list of every available property.
  • 3. Commercial Structuring: Negotiate and structure commercial terms for the shortlisted options. Factors: cost per sqft/seat, security deposit, lock-in & notice period, upfront cost, regular opex cost, annual escalation. This is the step where myHQ's scale and relationships with landlords/operators directly translate into a better deal than the client could get on their own.
  • 4. Compliance, Interiors, Legal Advisory: Double-check all key compliance requirements and consult on interiors efficiency. Factors: fire norms, security compliances, ownership of property, office layout design, registration of office agreement. Skipping this step is how clients end up signing for a space that later fails a fire-safety audit or has an ownership dispute.
  • 5. Transition Period Management: If the new office will take time, support the client through that period. Factors: current office terms, temporary office solution (if needed), regular check-ups on project pace and timeline. A managed office or lease build-out can take weeks to months — this step keeps the client operational in the gap.
  • 6. Move-in Date & Month: Help with the inauguration and any minor issues that come up in the first 1–2 weeks. Factors: inauguration setup, branding in office/building, minor setup issues (interiors/infrastructure). Our job isn't done at signing — we stay involved until the client is actually settled in.
Discovery → Shortlist → Structuring → Compliance → Transition → Move-in. Know this process cold — every AM transaction follows it.

04

Client Journey & Tools

Every AM lead moves through five stages in the CRM, and a consultant should always know exactly which stage each of their leads is sitting in:

  • Lead Posted: A new lead is posted into the system — from the myHQ website (our lead generation channel) or other inbound/outbound sources. Nothing happens to a lead until it's logged here; an enquiry that isn't posted into the system effectively doesn't exist for tracking purposes.
  • Lead Qualified: The qualification team reviews and qualifies the lead — checking that the requirement (city, team size, budget, timeline) is real and specific enough to act on, before it's handed to a sales consultant.
  • Options Shared: The sales point of contact shares workspace options — this is effectively the output of the Strategic Discovery and Curated Shortlist steps from the consulting process.
  • Visit Scheduled: The client schedules a visit to the workspace. This is the moment a lead becomes a serious prospect rather than a browsing enquiry.
  • Contract + Booking Done: The workspace is successfully booked by the client — at this point the Commercial Structuring and Compliance steps have already closed out.

The AM tech product has two parts: the myHQ Website (desktop + mobile), which is our lead generation channel — it has served 5,000+ clients and counting — and our Internal CRM, which works in tandem with Zoho CRM. The team manages leads and tracks progress here, and can share proposals directly from the CRM. Keeping the CRM up to date isn't optional admin work — it's how the qualification team, the sales consultant, and management all stay aligned on where every single deal stands.

Lead → Qualify → Options → Visit → Book. The myHQ Website brings leads in; the Internal + Zoho CRM is where you move them through to close.
