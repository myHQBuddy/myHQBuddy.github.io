Commerce is the engine of economic activity. Commercial real estate is where that activity takes physical shape — offices, retail, logistics, industrial parks, and business infrastructure. Every asset reflects how companies operate, expand, and create value.
Transaction Model 01
Sale / Purchase
Outright ownership transfer of a commercial asset — for investment, self-use, or long-term capital appreciation. The buyer becomes the landlord.
Typical buyers: REITs, family offices, HNIs, corporates acquiring campuses
Asset classes: Office parks, retail plazas, warehouses, industrial land
Requires large upfront capital — most companies choose to lease instead
Yields: Grade A office assets trade at 7–9% cap rates in India (2024)
Transaction Model 02 — The Dominant Model
Leasing / Rental
Occupying commercial space through a contractual agreement — without ownership. Lower upfront capital, operational flexibility, scalability. The primary model for 90%+ of commercial space decisions.

Flex Operators — The Space-as-a-Service Layer
Who takes raw space and turns it into a managed product
How it works
Flex operators sign conventional leases from developers (typically 5–7 year terms at Grade A rents), invest ₹800–1,500/sf in fit-out, and re-license individual desks, suites, and managed floors to end clients on 1–12 month terms. They absorb the capex risk — the client benefits from flexibility.
The operator economics
An operator paying ₹80/sf base rent fits out at ₹1,200/sf, depreciates the capex over 5 years, and licenses at ₹250/sf equivalent (₹10,000/seat at 40 sf/seat). At 80% occupancy → 30–40% gross margin. Below 60%, they break even. Below 50%, they bleed cash. This is why operators have structural urgency to fill seats — and why they list on myHQ.

myHQ sits at the intersection of two worlds — the advisory credibility of IPCs and DPCs (institutional and domestic property consultants) and the inventory transparency of listing platforms. Neither alone solves the client's problem at scale. myHQ does both.
